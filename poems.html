Living for Today

Dirty dreams that led you to eloquence
Will no more be there.
You speak but you can not perform.
The beautiful polish was just a garnish
But there wasn’t anything to decorate.
An emerald without the neck.
A stunning house made up of paper,
That flies with the slightest of wind.
A chinaware inside the display case,
That mom saves for the guest.
The extra large coat we got as children,
For a few more winters.
The fruits that are just about to rot,
we ate them today,
Saving the fresh ones for tomorrow,
Just to find them rotten as well.

No, dear lord.
We would rather have the clay, than the ceramics
We would rather have a neck and try on cheap jewels.
We would rather have an old wooden home.
We would rather dine in our chinaware.
We would rather get clothes that fit.
We would rather buy less and eat fresh.
We would rather dream than organize.
We would rather live today.

Shame

The shame in not loving
Can’t be the same as in loving
There are prophecies made
But each of us falls in a different way
We all are martyrs,
Some happy some sad
Some become martys even without knowing they died.

Give me a rhythm of ignorance
Pass me a naive gesture
Show me the oceans you have saved for me
I need to know
The ocean won't turn into a tsunami someday
Make me live in your darkness
So I get habituated
Stab me with the knife in your pocket
Someday, you are gonna do it anyway.

I am very strong.
But I need not be strong in love.
I need strength saved for some other compromise.
In Love,
I need to bleed and heal
And feel the shame again.

The Bridge

In the world of may bes and Ifs
Life is grey
But for bridges like me
Its either this side or across
A suicide or a dive
Kill or die
Danger lurks in the middle
Hanging by thread
Like love and promises.

All dried up last winter
There is no river beneath me
With merely a plain piece of abyss
The longer as I stare at it
It glances back to me in peace.

Happy enough to carry people to their destiny
Sad enough to not have one of my own.
I am a bridge in pain.
Ready as they decide coming back
To be wrecked all over again.

The song of solitude

The Pain subdues After a while, it stops scaring you
The constantly changing changes
Makes life it’s second degree derivative
Yet, you get through it,
As you have been flying higher.

Who did you expect to fight your battles?
That’s not a battle for anybody else
Just another ridiculous life choice.
For a while, there are people who assure
But nobody has your demons,
Nobody has your dreams
Most of them don’t have a dream to begin with.

Everyday you think, this is the lowest it gets
The next day life stoops even lower.
Who sets the ground for tolerance?
An old man once told you,
“Your pain is my water, I drink it everyday
Your expectations are my privilege, I yearn for it.
Your pleasure must be what heaven looks like”.

People, they are wonderful
As long as you don’t keep them around.
People drag people to their habits
There is no Individuality in groups.

You have got a rusty soul to begin with.
Nobody greases their hands for nothing.
In this ocean of solitude,
A drop of fire extinguishes,
A glint of ray hesitates
A gesture of kindness disappears overnight.

For this ocean of solitude,
Which all of us begin after a point
Submerges you to grow your wings,
And call it a boon or a curse,
The winner always stands alone.


Generation Gap

When things were scarce,
I learnt to save
When they were not
I still saved out of habit.
I was troubled if
I saved it all for my child
Things would never be scarce
And he would never save anything.

As the child grew,
He never looked upon me,
Synced instead of saving
The boy sat in a desk all day
No business etiquettes
Later that day
He went to the store
And brought me milk with cryptocurrency.

Somwhere Inbetween

People love the boldness of Bryan Adams.
Or elsewhere seek the sweetness of Taylor.
Who would listen to my voice?
That lingers somewhere in between.

They die to acquire Gates´wealth.
And pity on the people of somalia.
Who would appreciate my fortune?
That lingers somewhere in between.

The toppers were awarded.
The failures were criticized.
Nobody noticed my mark,
That lingers somewhere in between.

People gasp at a coitus.
They take flirts and crushes yet so lightly.
Who would talk of my love?
That lingers somewhere in between.

God Doesn't Play Dice With The Universe

Two strangers!
miles away
Unaware of each other
unlike choices, that wouldn't change for anyone
separate ambitions yet to be dovetailed.
It was long before
God, Dice or Universe even existed.
Two faces
inches apart
seemingly aware of each other's belongingness.
They exchanged a lot of
contract papers
It was around then
God decided playing Dice with the Universe.
Two troubled souls
Dreams turning to nightmares.
Both could only smell a fishy business
with no apparent benefits,
Something else was enticing.
The moment Love turned to luxury,
God decided not to play Dice with the Universe.

Oneself

We all have sorcerers head
Every head has some flesh
All fleshes there have wings
See, the inside might be a mess
Look harder where the nightingale sings
Of all eternal things
deepest lies the grace
During the dark dreary days
The bird disappears
Takes away the wings
Only feathers flutter
The reminisce of dread
and sonnet of death it rings
Inside the little head.

Do you dare spread them?
The entanglements
Create dots out of nothing
Chant hymn out of curses
Get life out of corpses
See ripples in the calm
Stories unfold a chapter
The rock once lit fire
The fire lit fires thereafter
Rocks still wait in silence
To burn civilizations.

I’ve met men
Limited by fear
I’ve met children
Limited by men
I’ve met women
Limited by children
I met me, ignored
I met me again
This time I wept to myself
Bitter black tears of acceptance
The fear crumbled forever
I never had to meet me again.
